Jewelry Care Tips

Keep your precious jewelry beautiful and lasting forever

Expert Care Guide

Gold Jewelry Care

  • Clean regularly with warm soapy water and soft brush
  • Rinse thoroughly and dry with soft lint-free cloth
  • Store separately to prevent scratching against other pieces
  • Remove before swimming, exercising, or household cleaning
  • Avoid contact with perfumes, lotions, and hairsprays
  • Polish occasionally with jewelry polishing cloth
  • Have professional cleaning done annually
  • Check settings and clasps regularly for wear

Pro Tip

22K gold is softer than 18K gold, so handle with extra care. The higher the karat, the more pure gold content, making it more susceptible to scratches and dents.

Diamond Care

  • Clean with ammonia solution (1:6 ratio with water)
  • Use soft toothbrush to clean behind the stone
  • Soak for 30 minutes, then rinse with clean water
  • Dry with lint-free cloth or air dry
  • Store each piece in separate soft pouch
  • Avoid touching diamonds with fingers (oils reduce brilliance)
  • Professional cleaning every 6 months recommended
  • Check prongs and settings regularly for security

Expert Advice

Diamonds are oil-attracting stones. Even clean hands leave oils that reduce their sparkle. Use a soft brush to clean the pavilion (bottom) of the diamond for maximum brilliance.

Silver Jewelry Care

  • Clean with silver polish or baking soda paste
  • Use soft cloth for polishing, avoid paper towels
  • Store in airtight containers with anti-tarnish strips
  • Wear regularly - skin oils help prevent tarnishing
  • Remove before showering or swimming
  • Keep away from rubber bands and elastic
  • Use chalk or silica gel packets in storage
  • Professional cleaning for heavily tarnished pieces

Warning

Never use toothpaste or baking soda on silver-plated jewelry as it can remove the plating. Always use proper silver cleaning products.

Pearl Care

  • Wipe with damp soft cloth after each wear
  • Store flat in soft pouches, never hang
  • Keep away from perfumes, cosmetics, and hair products
  • Put on pearls last when dressing
  • Restring annually if worn frequently
  • Use mild soap solution for deeper cleaning
  • Allow to air dry completely before storing
  • Avoid ultrasonic cleaners and steam cleaning

Important

Pearls are organic and delicate. Acids from perfumes, skin, and cosmetics can damage their nacre permanently. Always put pearls on last and take them off first.

Temple Jewelry Care

  • Handle delicate kemp stones with extra care
  • Clean gold parts with soft brush and mild soap
  • Avoid water contact with kemp and uncut stones
  • Store in individual cloth bags
  • Keep away from direct sunlight
  • Use dry cloth for cleaning intricate designs
  • Professional restoration for antique pieces
  • Check for loose stones before wearing

Traditional Care

Temple jewelry often contains natural materials like kemp stones that are sensitive to moisture. Clean only with dry methods and store in cool, dry places.

Antique Jewelry Care

  • Minimal cleaning to preserve patina and character
  • Use soft dry brush for dust removal
  • Professional evaluation before any restoration
  • Store in stable temperature and humidity
  • Document condition with photographs
  • Avoid harsh chemicals and ultrasonic cleaners
  • Handle minimally and with clean hands
  • Insure valuable pieces properly

Preservation

Antique jewelry value lies partly in its original condition. Over-cleaning or restoration can significantly reduce its historical and monetary value.

Step-by-Step Cleaning Guide

1

Prepare

Gather mild soap, warm water, soft brush, and lint-free cloth. Remove jewelry and work in good lighting.

2

Soak

Immerse jewelry in warm soapy water for 15-30 minutes. Use mild dish soap, avoid harsh detergents.

3

Brush Gently

Use soft toothbrush to clean crevices and behind stones. Be extra gentle with delicate settings.

4

Rinse

Rinse thoroughly under lukewarm running water. Ensure all soap residue is removed completely.

5

Dry

Pat dry with soft lint-free cloth. Air dry completely before storing to prevent moisture damage.

6

Polish

Use jewelry polishing cloth for final shine. Store properly in individual pouches or compartments.

Proper Storage Solutions

Individual Pouches

Store each piece in separate soft fabric pouches to prevent scratching and tangling.

Climate Control

Keep jewelry in cool, dry places. Avoid bathrooms and areas with temperature fluctuations.

Secure Storage

Use jewelry boxes with locks or safety deposit boxes for valuable pieces and when traveling.

Anti-Tarnish

Include anti-tarnish strips or chalk in storage containers to absorb moisture and prevent tarnishing.

Light Protection

Keep jewelry away from direct sunlight which can fade gemstones and damage certain materials.

Documentation

Keep receipts, certificates, and photos for insurance purposes and authenticity verification.
