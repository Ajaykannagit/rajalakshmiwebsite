Certificates & Hallmarks

Understanding authenticity and quality assurance in jewelry

Quality Certification

BIS Hallmark

The Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) hallmark is the most trusted certification for gold jewelry purity in India. It ensures that your jewelry meets the stated gold content standards.

  • Mandatory for all gold jewelry above 2 grams
  • Verifies exact purity levels (14K, 18K, 22K, 24K)
  • Includes unique identification number
  • Assay center details for verification
  • Jeweler's identification mark
  • Year of hallmarking for traceability

Guarantee

All our gold jewelry comes with authentic BIS hallmarking, ensuring you get exactly what you pay for.

Diamond Certificates

Diamond certificates from internationally recognized laboratories provide detailed analysis of the 4Cs - Cut, Color, Clarity, and Carat weight - ensuring authenticity and value.

  • GIA (Gemological Institute of America) certificates
  • IGI (International Gemological Institute) reports
  • Detailed 4C grading analysis
  • Unique laser inscription on diamond
  • Authenticity verification through lab
  • Insurance and resale value support

Expert Tip

A certified diamond retains its value better and provides confidence in your investment. Always ask for certification when purchasing diamonds.

Gemstone Certificates

Precious gemstones require specialized certification to verify their natural origin, treatments, and quality characteristics.

  • Natural vs. synthetic identification
  • Treatment disclosure (heating, irradiation, etc.)
  • Origin determination for premium stones
  • Color grading and clarity assessment
  • Carat weight and dimensions
  • Authenticity guarantee

Important

Treated gemstones are common and acceptable, but must be disclosed. Natural, untreated gems command premium prices.

Antique Jewelry Authentication

Antique and vintage jewelry requires specialized authentication to verify age, origin, and historical significance.

  • Age verification and period identification
  • Maker's marks and signature analysis
  • Historical significance assessment
  • Condition and restoration documentation
  • Provenance and ownership history
  • Market valuation for insurance

Heritage Value

Proper authentication adds both monetary and historical value to antique pieces, preserving their legacy for future generations.

Understanding BIS Hallmarks

BIS

BIS Logo

The official Bureau of Indian Standards logo certifying that the jewelry has been tested and verified for purity.

916

Purity Number

Indicates the purity of gold in parts per thousand. 916 means 22K gold (91.6% pure gold content).

A/C

Assay Center

Letter code identifying the BIS-approved assay center where the jewelry was tested and verified.

JM

Jeweler's Mark

Unique identification mark of the jeweler or manufacturer, providing traceability and accountability.

23

Year of Marking

Last two digits of the year when the jewelry was hallmarked, helping track the manufacturing period.

ID

Unique ID

Six-digit alphanumeric code that can be verified online through the BIS website for authenticity.

How to Verify Authenticity

Check Hallmarks

Look for BIS logo, purity number, assay center mark, jeweler's identification, and year of marking on your jewelry.

Online Verification

Visit the official BIS website and enter the unique 6-digit alphanumeric code to verify hallmark authenticity.

BIS Care App

Download the official BIS Care mobile app to scan QR codes and verify hallmarks instantly using your smartphone.

Professional Testing

For additional assurance, get your jewelry tested at any BIS-approved assay center for independent verification.

Certificate Verification

For diamonds and gemstones, verify certificates directly with the issuing laboratory using their online verification systems.

Expert Consultation

Consult with certified gemologists or experienced jewelers for professional opinion on authenticity and quality.

Important Tips for Buyers

Red Flags to Avoid

  • Jewelry without BIS hallmarking
  • Suspiciously low prices for stated purity
  • Illegible or tampered hallmarks
  • Sellers reluctant to show certificates
  • Missing or fake gemstone certificates
  • No return or exchange policy
  • Pressure tactics for immediate purchase
  • No physical store address or documentation

Best Practices

  • Always ask for BIS hallmarked jewelry
  • Verify hallmarks before purchase
  • Request original certificates for diamonds/gems
  • Keep all purchase receipts and documents
  • Buy from reputable, established jewelers
  • Get proper insurance valuation
  • Understand return and exchange policies
  • Take photos of hallmarks for records
